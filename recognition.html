Awards and Recognition

My research has been recognized across aerospace engineering, mechanical engineering, structural mechanics, and advanced manufacturing. The awards and external recognition listed below reflect contributions to nonlinear aeroelasticity, multifunctional flexible structures, additive manufacturing, wind-tunnel testing, and mechanical metamaterials.

Awards

Best Presentation Award in Aerospace Numerical Simulation Technology

Selected in 2026; award ceremony and invited award presentation planned for 2027
58th Fluid Dynamics Conference / 44th Aerospace Numerical Simulation Symposium
FDC/ANSS 2026

Selected as the best presentation in the Aerospace Numerical Simulation Technology category for the presentation:

“Spatial Mapping of Flutter Excitation and Dissipation Based on Aerodynamic Component Decomposition in Potential Flow”

The research develops a spatially resolved aerodynamic-work diagnostic framework by decomposing potential-flow aerodynamic forces into their underlying mechanical contributions. The resulting energy maps identify the regions and mechanisms that drive or dissipate flutter, providing a physics-based basis for flutter diagnosis and aeroelastic tailoring.

The award ceremony and award presentation are planned to be held during the next FDC/ANSS conference, scheduled for June 30–July 2, 2027, in Tokyo.

International Research Experience

My research has been developed through collaborations and research activities spanning universities, public research institutes, and industry in Japan, the United States, and Europe.

NASA-Related Flexible-Structure Sensing Research

As a Graduate Research Assistant at the University of Alabama, I contributed to NASA-supported research on strain-based deformation and vibration monitoring for flexible launch-vehicle structures. The work developed sensor-instrumented reference structures and reconstruction methods for estimating multi-axial bending vibration from distributed strain measurements.

German Aerospace Center (DLR)

As a Guest Scientist at the DLR Institute of Aeroelasticity from September 2023 to August 2024, I conducted research on transonic wind-tunnel testing, structural shape identification, and strain-based deformation monitoring for additively manufactured aeroelastic wing models.

JAXA

My research at the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency has addressed nonlinear aeroelasticity, metal-additive-manufactured wind-tunnel models, transonic flutter testing, advanced sensing, and experimental validation. I continue this collaboration as a Visiting Researcher in the Aviation Technology Directorate.
